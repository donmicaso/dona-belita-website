About Micka Stephano Mavoa

Micka Stephano Mavoa is not only the narrator of The Rise and Fall of Dona Belita, but also the son of Bertha Riziki Mavoa, whose life story he tells with heartfelt dedication. Entrusted with the responsibility of completing his mother’s memoir, Micka captures her resilience, courage, and the hardships she endured.

As the fourth child in a family of five, Micka played an essential role in supporting his mother and siblings, particularly during her illness. His involvement in the family’s journey—from securing his mother’s medical care to preserving her legacy—gives him a unique perspective on her life.

Today, Micka continues to honor his mother's memory, using her story as a source of inspiration for others. Through his work, he hopes to support women facing similar struggles and encourage resilience in the face of adversity.

Stay Tuned for More

Micka is currently working on his next book, The Making of Don Micaso, which will delve into his own life journey, struggles, and the commitment to family that he inherited from his mother.
